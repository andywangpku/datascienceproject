DATA AND SCOPE

The Global Database of Events, Language, and Tone (GDELT) is a database on human societal-scale behaviour and beliefs across all countries of the world since 1979.

GDELT is comprehensive and yet has a precision down to city level, and provides daily updates to make it almost real time.

This is a case study into Georgia. We want to leverage big data analysis and visualizaiotn tools, to look for potential insights in international relationship and domestic events.

This project serves as an experiment of GDELT data in international affairs related research.

event prediction

Can we predict what will happen in the future?

We want to predict for some of the most important events, such as fights, protests.

bar chart for fights since 1998
- bar height means number of fights in a month
- upper chart is reality, lower chart is prediction
- green means correct direction,red means wrong one

For any event, to predict the exact quantity is very difficult. Predicting the trend is good enough for better preparation on bad events and give UN officials time to step in.

"Correct direction" means we successfully predicted the increment or decrement of fights.

Our model is an ARMA(2,3) time series model. We used previous months data to predict for the next month.

Tone Analysis

"Tone" of an event is about its sentiment, importance and impact.

Can we predict the average tone ( aggregated public opinions ) for the next month?

Tone analysis and prediction can help officials to better understand public opinions and try to prepare for potential social unrest.

We created a new feature to describe the tone and analysed monthly average tone since 2008

line chart
- bold line is actual average tone
- shaded area is margin between prediction and reality
- green for underestimate, orange for overestimate
bar chart
- monthly increment and decrement in average tone
- upper one is actual, lower one is predicted
- correct in direction ~ 70%

We built a time series model with auto regression, using previous 6 months tone to predict for the next month.
